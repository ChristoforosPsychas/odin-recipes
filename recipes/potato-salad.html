Potato Salad

A tasteful potato salad

This potato salad recipe is for the traditional creamy type of potato salad, with eggs, celery, and relish. It's perfect for making ahead to let the flavors develop.

Ingredients

  • Potatoes: It's not potato salad without potatoes. The recipe doesn't specify what size potatoes or what kind, so choose medium-size potatoes, or enough to serve eight. (5 potatoes)
  • Eggs: Hard-boiled eggs are a must-have for this kind of old-fashioned potato salad, but you can leave them out if you really want to.(3 eggs)
  • Celery: Celery adds a crunchy element to balance the softer texture and creaminess of the potatoes.(1 cup chopped celery)
  • Onion: You can use any onion you like, including yellow, white, or red onions. Chop the onion into small, uniform pieces so no one bites into a big chunk of raw onion.(½ cup chopped onion)
  • Relish: Use your favorite sweet pickle relish. Some reviewers chopped up sweet gherkin pickles, or substitute relish or pickles that are less sweet.(½ cup sweet pickle relish)
  • Mayonnaise: This is the creamy element that brings all the ingredients together. For best results, mix the mayonnaise, mustard, relish, and seasonings together in a separate bowl. Give it a taste test and adjust the seasonings before adding to the potatoes, eggs, and onion.(¼ cup mayonnaise)
  • Mustard: Use your favorite mustard. Some reviewers like to use yellow mustard and some prefer Dijon.(1 tablespoon prepared mustard)
  • Seasonings: This recipe calls for garlic salt, celery salt, and ground black pepper to taste; some reviewers added extras like paprika and garlic powder. You can add minced herbs, too, like dill, chives, parsley, and tarragon. (¼ teaspoon garlic salt, ¼ teaspoon celery salt, ground black pepper to taste)

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add potatoes and cook until tender but still firm, about 15 minutes.
  3. Drain, cool, peel, and chop potatoes.
  4. While potatoes cook, place eggs in a saucepan and cover with cold water. Bring water to a boil; cover, remove from heat, and let eggs stand in hot water for 10 to 12 minutes.
  5. Remove from hot water, cool, peel, and chop eggs.
  6. Combine the potatoes, eggs, celery, onion, relish, mayonnaise, mustard, garlic salt, celery salt, and pepper in a large bowl. Mix together well and refrigerate until chilled.
  7. Enjoy!
